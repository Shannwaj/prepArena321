Physics Medium

Kinematics: Projectile Motion

A projectile is launched with an initial velocity of 20 m/s at an angle of 45° with the horizontal. Calculate the maximum height reached and the time of flight.

Solution:

1. Maximum height (H) = (v₀²sin²θ)/(2g)

2. Time of flight (T) = (2v₀sinθ)/g

3. Substituting values:

H = (20² × sin²45°)/(2 × 9.8) = 10.2 m

T = (2 × 20 × sin45°)/9.8 = 2.89 s

Detailed Explanation:

1. For maximum height, we use the vertical component of velocity

2. The time of flight considers both upward and downward motion

3. Key concepts: projectile motion, conservation of energy

Chemistry Hard

Chemical Equilibrium

For the reaction N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g), if Kp = 1.5 × 10⁻⁵ at 400°C, calculate the equilibrium constant Kc.

Solution:

1. Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn

2. Δn = (2) - (1 + 3) = -2

3. T = 400°C = 673 K

4. Kc = Kp/(RT)^Δn

5. Kc = 1.5 × 10⁻⁵/(0.0821 × 673)^(-2)

6. Kc = 2.8 × 10⁴

Detailed Explanation:

1. Relationship between Kp and Kc

2. Effect of temperature on equilibrium

3. Significance of Δn in gas phase reactions

Mathematics Medium

Differential Equations

Solve the differential equation: dy/dx + 2y = e⁻²ˣ, given y(0) = 1

Solution:

1. This is a linear differential equation

2. Integrating factor = e^∫2dx = e²ˣ

3. Multiplying both sides by e²ˣ:

e²ˣ(dy/dx) + 2e²ˣy = 1

4. d/dx(ye²ˣ) = 1

5. ye²ˣ = x + C

6. y = (x + C)e⁻²ˣ

7. Using y(0) = 1: C = 1

8. Final solution: y = (x + 1)e⁻²ˣ

Detailed Explanation:

1. Method of integrating factors

2. Solving first-order linear differential equations

3. Initial value problems
