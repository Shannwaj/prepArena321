Practice Questions

Biology

Cell Biology

Which organelle is known as the powerhouse of the cell and why?

Solution:

  1. Mitochondria is known as the powerhouse of the cell
  2. It produces ATP through cellular respiration
  3. Contains inner and outer membranes
  4. Has its own DNA and ribosomes
Biology

Genetics

In a dihybrid cross between AABB × aabb, what will be the phenotypic ratio in F2 generation?

Solution:

  1. F1 generation will be AaBb (all heterozygous)
  2. F2 will show independent assortment
  3. Phenotypic ratio: 9:3:3:1
  4. This demonstrates Mendel's Law of Independent Assortment
Biology

Plant Physiology

What is the role of abscisic acid in plants?

Solution:

  1. Promotes seed dormancy
  2. Causes stomatal closure during water stress
  3. Inhibits growth
  4. Promotes leaf senescence and abscission
Biology

Human Physiology

Explain the process of muscle contraction with reference to the sliding filament theory.

Solution:

  1. Calcium ions bind to troponin
  2. Tropomyosin moves, exposing binding sites on actin
  3. Myosin heads bind to actin forming cross-bridges
  4. ATP hydrolysis powers the power stroke
  5. Sarcomere shortens causing contraction
Chemistry

Organic Chemistry

What is the major product formed when ethanol reacts with concentrated H₂SO₄ at 443K?

Solution:

  1. This is a dehydration reaction
  2. CH₃CH₂OH → CH₂=CH₂ + H₂O
  3. Major product: Ethene
  4. H₂SO₄ acts as a dehydrating agent
Chemistry

Chemical Bonding

Why is the boiling point of water higher than that of H₂S despite both having similar molecular mass?

Solution:

  1. Water molecules form hydrogen bonds
  2. H₂S molecules have weak van der Waals forces
  3. Hydrogen bonds are stronger than van der Waals forces
  4. More energy required to break H-bonds in water
Chemistry

Coordination Chemistry

What is the IUPAC name of [Co(NH₃)₄Cl₂]Cl?

Solution:

  1. Co is in +3 oxidation state
  2. 4 NH₃ ligands and 2 Cl⁻ ligands are coordinated
  3. 1 Cl⁻ is outside the coordination sphere
  4. IUPAC name: Tetraamminedichlorocobalt(III) chloride
Physics

Human Eye

Calculate the power of a corrective lens needed for a person whose far point is 80cm.

Solution:

  1. For myopia, power = -1/far point (in meters)
  2. Far point = 80cm = 0.8m
  3. Power = -1/0.8
  4. Power = -1.25 D
Physics

Wave Optics

In Young's double-slit experiment, if the wavelength of light is 500nm and the distance between slits is 0.1mm, calculate the fringe width when the screen is 1m away.

Solution:

  1. Fringe width β = λD/d
  2. λ = 500 × 10⁻⁹m
  3. D = 1m
  4. d = 0.1 × 10⁻³m
  5. β = (500 × 10⁻⁹ × 1)/(0.1 × 10⁻³)
  6. β = 5 × 10⁻³m = 5mm
Physics

Modern Physics

Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of an electron accelerated through a potential difference of 100V.

Solution:

  1. λ = h/√(2meV)
  2. h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ Js
  3. m = 9.1 × 10⁻³¹ kg
  4. e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
  5. V = 100V
  6. λ = 1.23 × 10⁻¹⁰m
