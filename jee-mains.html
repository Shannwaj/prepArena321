Practice Questions

Physics

Kinematics

A stone is thrown vertically upward with a velocity of 49 m/s. Calculate the maximum height reached by the stone. (g = 9.8 m/s²)

Solution:

  1. Using v² = u² + 2gh where v = 0 at max height
  2. 0 = (49)² + 2(-9.8)h
  3. h = 49²/(2 × 9.8) = 122.5 meters
Physics

Newton's Laws

A block of mass 2 kg is placed on a rough horizontal surface with coefficient of friction 0.5. Find the force required to move the block. (g = 9.8 m/s²)

Solution:

  1. Normal force N = mg = 2 × 9.8 = 19.6 N
  2. Friction force f = μN = 0.5 × 19.6
  3. Required force = 9.8 N
Physics

Circular Motion

A particle moves in a circle of radius 2m with a constant speed of 4m/s. Calculate its centripetal acceleration.

Solution:

  1. Centripetal acceleration a = v²/r
  2. a = (4)²/2
  3. a = 8 m/s²
Physics

Work and Energy

A 2kg block slides down a frictionless inclined plane of height 5m. Calculate its velocity at the bottom.

Solution:

  1. Using conservation of energy: mgh = ½mv²
  2. 2 × 9.8 × 5 = ½ × 2 × v²
  3. v = √(2gh) = √(2 × 9.8 × 5)
  4. v = 9.9 m/s
Chemistry

Mole Concept

Calculate the number of moles in 5.85g of sodium chloride (NaCl). [Atomic mass: Na = 23, Cl = 35.5]

Solution:

  1. Molar mass of NaCl = 23 + 35.5 = 58.5 g/mol
  2. Number of moles = Given mass/Molar mass
  3. = 5.85/58.5 = 0.1 moles
Chemistry

Chemical Equilibrium

For the reaction N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃, if the equilibrium constant Kc = 8, what is the value of Kp at 400K?

Solution:

  1. Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn
  2. Δn = 2 - (1 + 3) = -2
  3. Kp = 8(0.0821 × 400)^(-2)
  4. Kp = 0.0366
Chemistry

Electrochemistry

Calculate the mass of copper deposited when a current of 2 amperes is passed through CuSO₄ solution for 30 minutes. (Atomic mass of Cu = 63.5, F = 96500 C/mol)

Solution:

  1. Time in seconds = 30 × 60 = 1800s
  2. Charge passed = Current × Time = 2 × 1800 = 3600C
  3. Mass = (Atomic mass × Current × Time)/(Valency × F)
  4. Mass = (63.5 × 3600)/(2 × 96500) = 1.18g
Chemistry

Chemical Kinetics

The half-life of a first-order reaction is 20 minutes. Calculate the rate constant.

Solution:

  1. For first-order reaction: t₁/₂ = 0.693/k
  2. 20 = 0.693/k
  3. k = 0.693/20
  4. k = 0.03465 min⁻¹
Mathematics

Calculus

Find the derivative of f(x) = x·sin(x)

Solution:

  1. Using product rule: d/dx[u·v] = u·dv/dx + v·du/dx
  2. Here, u = x and v = sin(x)
  3. f'(x) = x·cos(x) + sin(x)
Mathematics

Integration

Evaluate ∫(x²+1)dx from 0 to 2

Solution:

  1. ∫(x²+1)dx = (x³/3 + x)
  2. Upper limit: (8/3 + 2)
  3. Lower limit: (0 + 0)
  4. Result = 14/3
Mathematics

Vectors

Find the angle between vectors a = 2i + j + 2k and b = i + 2j + k

Solution:

  1. cos θ = (a·b)/(|a|·|b|)
  2. a·b = 2(1) + 1(2) + 2(1) = 6
  3. |a| = √(4 + 1 + 4) = 3
  4. |b| = √(1 + 4 + 1) = √6
  5. cos θ = 6/(3√6)
  6. θ = 35.26°
Physics

Thermodynamics

A heat engine operates between 400K and 300K. Calculate its maximum possible efficiency.

Solution:

  1. Maximum efficiency = 1 - T₂/T₁
  2. = 1 - 300/400
  3. = 1 - 0.75
  4. = 0.25 or 25%
Chemistry

Organic Chemistry

Predict the major product when propene reacts with HBr in the presence of peroxide.

Solution:

  1. This is anti-Markovnikov addition (peroxide effect)
  2. CH₃-CH=CH₂ + HBr → CH₃-CH₂-CH₂Br
  3. Major product: 1-bromopropane
  4. Mechanism: Free radical addition
Mathematics

Complex Numbers

Find all values of z satisfying z⁴ = 16

Solution:

  1. z = ⁴√16 × e^(2πik/4), k = 0,1,2,3
  2. |z| = 2
  3. z₁ = 2 (k=0)
  4. z₂ = 2i (k=1)
  5. z₃ = -2 (k=2)
  6. z₄ = -2i (k=3)
Physics

Electrostatics

Two point charges of 2μC and -2μC are placed 3cm apart. Calculate the electric field at the midpoint of the line joining them.

Solution:

  1. Distance from each charge to midpoint = 1.5cm
  2. E = k(q₁/r² - q₂/r²)
  3. E = (9×10⁹)(2×10⁻⁶)/(0.015)² - (-2×10⁻⁶)/(0.015)²
  4. E = 0 N/C (fields cancel out)
Chemistry

Coordination Chemistry

Calculate the magnetic moment of [Fe(H₂O)₆]³⁺ ion (Atomic number of Fe = 26)

Solution:

  1. Fe³⁺ has electronic configuration [Ar]3d⁵
  2. High spin complex: 5 unpaired electrons
  3. μ = √[n(n+2)] BM where n = number of unpaired electrons
  4. μ = √[5(5+2)] = √35 = 5.92 BM
Mathematics

Probability

A bag contains 4 red and 3 blue balls. Two balls are drawn at random. Find the probability that both are red.

Solution:

  1. Total number of ways = C(7,2) = 21
  2. Favorable outcomes = C(4,2) = 6
  3. Probability = 6/21
  4. = 2/7
Physics

Optics

A thin lens forms a real image of an object placed 30cm from it. If the image is twice the size of the object, find the focal length of the lens.

Solution:

  1. Magnification m = -v/u = 2
  2. If u = -30cm, then v = 60cm
  3. Using 1/f = 1/u + 1/v
  4. 1/f = -1/30 + 1/60
  5. f = 20cm
Chemistry

Chemical Bonding

Predict the hybridization and shape of XeF₄ molecule.

Solution:

  1. Xe has 8 valence electrons
  2. 4 electrons used in bonding with F
  3. 2 lone pairs
  4. Hybridization: sp³d²
  5. Shape: Square planar
Mathematics

Determinants

If A is a 3×3 matrix and |A| = 6, find |2A|.

Solution:

  1. For a 3×3 matrix, |kA| = k³|A|
  2. |2A| = 2³|A|
  3. |2A| = 8 × 6
  4. |2A| = 48
