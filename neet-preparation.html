Biology Medium

Cell Division: Mitosis

Describe the stages of mitosis and explain the significance of each stage in cell division.

Answer:

1. Prophase:

  • Chromosomes condense and become visible
  • Nuclear envelope breaks down
  • Spindle fibers begin to form

2. Metaphase:

  • Chromosomes align at the equatorial plate
  • Spindle fibers attach to centromeres

3. Anaphase:

  • Sister chromatids separate
  • Chromosomes move to opposite poles

4. Telophase:

  • Nuclear envelope reforms
  • Chromosomes decondense
  • Cytokinesis begins

Detailed Explanation:

1. Significance of each stage:

  • Prophase: Prepares chromosomes for division
  • Metaphase: Ensures proper chromosome alignment
  • Anaphase: Separates genetic material
  • Telophase: Completes nuclear division

2. Key concepts:

  • Chromosome condensation and organization
  • Spindle apparatus formation
  • Genetic material distribution
Physics Hard

Work, Energy, and Power

A block of mass 2 kg is pushed up an inclined plane of angle 30° with a force of 20 N. If the coefficient of friction is 0.2, calculate the work done in moving the block 5 m up the plane.

Solution:

1. Components of forces:

  • Normal force (N) = mg cosθ
  • Frictional force (f) = μN
  • Applied force (F) = 20 N

2. Calculations:

  • N = 2 × 9.8 × cos(30°) = 16.97 N
  • f = 0.2 × 16.97 = 3.39 N
  • Work done = (F - f) × d
  • Work done = (20 - 3.39) × 5 = 83.05 J

Detailed Explanation:

1. Key concepts:

  • Force components on inclined plane
  • Frictional force calculation
  • Work done against friction

2. Important points:

  • Consider both applied force and friction
  • Work is done against gravity and friction
  • Use proper sign conventions
Chemistry Medium

Chemical Bonding

Explain the formation of NH₃ molecule using valence bond theory and hybridization.

Answer:

1. Electronic configuration:

  • N: 1s² 2s² 2p³
  • H: 1s¹

2. Hybridization:

  • N undergoes sp³ hybridization
  • Forms four sp³ hybrid orbitals

3. Bond formation:

  • Three sp³ orbitals form σ bonds with H
  • One sp³ orbital contains lone pair

4. Molecular geometry:

  • Tetrahedral electron geometry
  • Trigonal pyramidal molecular geometry

Detailed Explanation:

1. Key concepts:

  • Valence bond theory
  • Hybridization
  • Molecular geometry

2. Important points:

  • Role of lone pair in geometry
  • Bond angle considerations
  • Polarity of the molecule
